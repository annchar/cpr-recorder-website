Information Collection and Use

Health and session data stored on your device

CPR Recorder is a CPR training and recording app. The app may store the following on your device:

  • CPR session records – including identifiers you assign (e.g. patient or case name), start and end times, duration, and related fields you enter (such as age and gender if you provide them)
  • CPR event data – messages and timestamps recorded during a session (e.g. rhythm checks, shocks, medications, CPR cycles)

How we use this data: This data is used to provide the recording features within the app (e.g. session history, summaries, timelines, and export).

Where this data is stored: This full session and event data is stored in the app’s local database on your device. I do not operate my own servers to store your full CPR records.

Analytics and diagnostics (third parties)

The app uses Google Firebase services. When you save a completed CPR record, the app may send a single analytics event to Google Analytics for Firebase. This event is designed not to include your record title/patient name or the full text of your CPR timeline.

The analytics event may include, for example:

  • Aggregated clinical-style metrics derived from the session (e.g. initial rhythm category, counts of shocks or CPR cycles, time-to-first metrics in seconds, total CPR duration in seconds, session duration in seconds, outcome category where applicable)
  • Optional fields you entered in the app (e.g. age and gender), and an approximate country/region derived from your device language or SIM settings (not precise GPS location)

Firebase may also process device and usage data according to Google’s policies. For crashes, Firebase Crashlytics may receive diagnostic information (e.g. stack traces) to help fix errors.

For how Google uses data, see the links under “Third-party services” below.
